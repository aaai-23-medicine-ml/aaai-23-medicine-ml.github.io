Advanced and Accessible Health Data Science with Rtemis
Efstathios D. Gennatas, MBBS AICSM PhD
University of California, San Francisco

Abstract:
Health-related data generated from basic research, electronic health records, and wearables, among others, is continuously proliferating. At the same time, the questions such data is used to answer are increasing in number and complexity. To address these, Health Data Science depends on effective collaboration across fields and disciplines. It requires:
1. Domain expertise in biomedical science/clinical medicine,
2. Quantitative expertise in Statistics/Machine Learning, and
3. Technical expertise in computer programming, data management, and more.
Domain-aware health data science software built to combine domain, quantitative, and technical expertise can help catalyze interdisciplinary work and the development of effective and efficient analysis pipelines.
Rtemis is a comprehensive health data science platform designed around that principle. It provides:
1. Visualization, including static and interactive graphics
2. Advanced procedures for statistics and machine learning, including supervised and unsupervised learning
The rtemis ecosystem is novel in providing both:
1. 'rtemis': A highly flexible and efficient API that allows programming access to advanced visualization and modeling
2. 'rtemislive': A web application providing a no-code, graphical user interface to 'rtemis'
The API and web application are accompanied by extensive documentation and can be used by data science experts and novice users alike. The modular and efficient design of rtemis makes it also well-suited for the development of novel ML algorithms. Algorithms developed in rtemis include the Additive Tree, the Linear Additive Tree, and Expert-Augmented Machine Learning.
Ongoing work includes expanding domain-specific functionality (e.g. in bioinformatics, medical imaging, epidemiology, etc.) as well as creating educational modules for health data science training.

A Short Bio:
Efstathios D. Gennatas, MBBS AICSM PhD, is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Epidemiology and Biostatistics, an affiliate at the Bakar Computational Health Science Institute, and a member of the Center for Intelligent Imaging at the University of California, San Francisco. He completed medical school at Imperial College London and doctoral training in Neuroscience at the University of Pennsylvania. His work includes applied data science in basic and clinical research as well as development of methods and software for biomedical data analysis, while he also teaches programming for health data science.
